Shared Migrations

Spaniards, Cubans, Dominicans & Venezuelans
Wealth flowed into Spain from sugar, tobacco, cocoa, and coffee grown in its Caribbean and Latin American colonies; while Spaniards flowed out of Spain to the colonies as sailors, soldiers, settlers, and government officials. Some stayed, intermarried with the local population, and raised generations of descendants who saw themselves as belonging to their new homes rather than Spain. Their revolutionary spirit would eventually free them from Spanish rule as they forged new countries, cultures, and national identities.
